The Ten Best Animated Movies Of All Time

The Prince of Egypt (1998)

From Dreamworks Animation

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

The Prince of Egypt is one of few animated movies that I don't hesitate to call a masterpiece. Its story focuses on the Biblical story of Moses.

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018)

From Sony Animation

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

They said it couldn't be done -- that an animated Spider-Man movie wouldn't be good. Sony knocked it out of the park. The Oscar was well-deserved.

Princess Mononoke (1997)

From Studio Ghibli

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

This movie inspired The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, and it shows. It is undeniably environmentalist and empathetic in its message.

Spirited Away (2001)

From Studio Ghibli

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

Spirited Away is yet another Oscar winner that deserves its accolades. It is beautiful and quintessentially Ghibli.

The Breadwinner (2017)

From Cartoon Saloon

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

This is a fairly indie pick for me. It is based off a children's book, and tells the story of a young girl in Afghanistan during the Taliban regime.

Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron (2002)

From Dreamworks Animation

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

You would think a movie about Matt Damon as a horse is set up for failure, but this movie is incredibly touching and has a bold anti-colonialist message.

Treasure Planet (2002)

From Disney Animation

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

I recently discovered this movie, and it is a crying shame that it was snubbed by audiences. This movie is a futuristic retelling of Treasure Island.

Anastasia (1997)

From Fox Animation Studios

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

This movie's costume design and music is leagues above many modern, more polished animated blockbusters. And as a child I always thought Dmitri was cute.

How to Train Your Dragon (2010)

From Dreamworks Animation

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

This movie, like many Dreamworks films, is a masterpiece. The soundtrack itself is a journey to itself. Also, dragons.

Brave (2012)

From Pixar Animation Studios

Link to the movie's IMDB page.

This movie gets too much hate. I'm going to say it -- I love Brave. You guys are just mean. It's beautiful, set in medieval Scotland, and has a beautiful mother-daughter relationship at the center.
